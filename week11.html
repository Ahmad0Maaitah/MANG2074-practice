🔬 Research Methods Lab — Research Sprint

The final week puts the whole module together: a real piece of empirical research is a pipeline — question → data → model class → diagnostics → interpretation — and the hard part is not running regressions, it is not fooling yourself. Below: concept cards for the methodology that examiners (and referees) actually test, seven interactive platforms (build a mini paper, manufacture false discoveries by p-hacking, stress-test a "finding" for robustness, translate statistics into economics, run a full specification curve, mine 50 trading signals and watch them die out of sample, and replicate a "published" result 200 times), a clinic of research pathologies, the Research Sprint game, and twelve code challenges spanning the whole module.

Platform 5 — Specification-curve lab

One dataset, one question — does factor X predict returns? — and four researcher degrees of freedom: trim outliers or not, which control set (none / A / A+B), which sample period (full / post-break half), winsorise or not. That is 2×3×2×2 = 24 defensible specifications. The lab runs ALL of them and plots the sorted estimates — the specification curve of Simonsohn, Simmons & Nelson — with the spec your toggles would have picked marked in gold.
