🕵️ Markov Switching — Regime Detective

Markets seem to live in regimes: long calm spells with small steady gains, punctuated by crisis spells with violent swings. A Markov-switching model formalises this: an unobserved state st ∈ {1, 2} follows a Markov chain with transition probabilities p11 and p22, and returns have regime-specific mean μs and volatility σs. This page gives you the parameter cards, four interactive platforms (simulate regimes, run the Hamilton filter live, dissect the transition matrix, and try to tell Markov switching from GARCH), a clinic of classic estimation problems, the Regime Detective game, and eight code challenges.

Platform 2 — Hamilton filter, live

The Hamilton filter is just Bayes' rule applied one observation at a time: predict where the chain probably is, then re-weight by how likely today's return is under each regime. Step through a simulated series (n = 150; μ₁ = 0.8, σ₁ = 2, μ₂ = −0.5, p₁₁ = 0.97 fixed) and watch the filtered probability update. At the end, the Kim smoother is overlaid so you can see what hindsight buys you.

Platform 4 — Regime vs GARCH face-off

Both Markov-switching variance and GARCH produce volatility clustering — but by different mechanisms: discrete jumps between two volatility levels vs smooth decay of a continuous variance. One button simulates one of each (matched unconditional variance, n = 350, zero means); your job is to tell which is which from the data alone.
