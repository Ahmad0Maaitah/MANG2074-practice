🕵️ Markov Switching — Regime Detective

Markets seem to live in regimes: long calm spells with small steady gains, punctuated by crisis spells with violent swings. A Markov-switching model formalises this: an unobserved state st ∈ {1, 2} follows a Markov chain with transition probabilities p11 and p22, and returns have regime-specific mean μs and volatility σs. Here you will simulate two-regime markets, learn what transition persistence does to regime durations, and then play detective: spot the hidden switch point from the data alone.

🔬 Simulation — the two-regime market

Returns are generated as rt = μst + σst·εt, εt ~ N(0,1), with st a 2-state Markov chain (n = 300 months). Drag the sliders and watch the market change character.
