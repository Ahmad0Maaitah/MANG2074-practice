⚖️ Panel Data — Hausman Court

Panel data follow the same firms over time: yit = αi + βxit + εit. The firm effect αi captures everything fixed about firm i — management quality, industry, culture. If αi is correlated with x, pooled OLS (and random effects) are biased; fixed effects (the "within" estimator) survives by using only variation inside each firm. Work through the parameter cards, seven interactive platforms (watch pooled OLS lie, animate the within transformation, stress-test the Hausman test by Monte Carlo, see why everyone clusters their standard errors, race five estimators in the arena, learn when entity FE alone fails under common shocks, and meet Nickell bias in dynamic panels), the problems clinic, then take the judge's seat in Hausman Court.

Platform 3 — Hausman lab (Monte Carlo)

The Hausman test compares β̂FE and β̂RE: H = (β̂FE−β̂RE)²/(V̂FE−V̂RE) ~ χ²₁ under H₀: corr(αi, x) = 0. But how good is it really? Pick a true correlation and panel dimensions, run 300 simulated panels, and look at the distribution of H with your own eyes.

Platform 7 — Dynamic panel & Nickell bias lab

Dynamic DGP: yit = γyi,t−1 + αi + εit, N = 30 firms. Estimate γ by fixed effects, 200 Monte-Carlo panels at your chosen T, then trace the mean bias across T = 4…40 against Nickell's approximation −(1+γ)/(T−1). Why FE fails here: demeaning subtracts ȳi, which contains every εit — so the demeaned lag is mechanically (negatively) correlated with the demeaned error. No amount of N fixes it; only T does.

What to notice: press Run. Every one of the 200 panels understates γ the same way — that is bias, not noise. The second chart shows it melting away only as T grows.
Remedies (name-check): Anderson–Hsiao — first-difference away αi, then instrument Δyi,t−1 with the level yi,t−2 (uncorrelated with Δεit). Arellano–Bond GMM — same idea industrialised: use ALL available deeper lags as instruments, weighted optimally (the standard tool for firm panels with T ≈ 5–15). Bias-corrected FE / just get more T — bias ∝ 1/T, so macro panels with T ≥ 30 mostly stop worrying.
