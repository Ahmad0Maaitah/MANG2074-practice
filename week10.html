⚖️ Panel Data — Hausman Court

Panel data follow the same firms over time: yit = αi + βxit + εit. The firm effect αi captures everything fixed about firm i — management quality, industry, culture. If αi is correlated with x, pooled OLS (and random effects) are biased; fixed effects (the "within" estimator) survives by using only variation inside each firm. Work through the parameter cards, four interactive platforms (watch pooled OLS lie, animate the within transformation, stress-test the Hausman test by Monte Carlo, and see why everyone clusters their standard errors), the problems clinic, then take the judge's seat in Hausman Court.

Platform 3 — Hausman lab (Monte Carlo)

The Hausman test compares β̂FE and β̂RE: H = (β̂FE−β̂RE)²/(V̂FE−V̂RE) ~ χ²₁ under H₀: corr(αi, x) = 0. But how good is it really? Pick a true correlation and panel dimensions, run 300 simulated panels, and look at the distribution of H with your own eyes.

What to notice: run the reps at corr = 0 first — the histogram should sit on the gold χ²₁ curve and reject about 5% of the time (correct size). Then raise the correlation and watch power appear.

Platform 4 — Cluster inference lab

Firm-level shocks persist: if a firm has a bad year, it tends to have a bad decade. That correlation makes naive OLS standard errors far too small.
